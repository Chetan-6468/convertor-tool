About Infix to Prefix/Postfix Converter

Welcome to the about page for the Infix to Prefix/Postfix Converter! This tool is designed to help you convert mathematical expressions from infix notation to either prefix or postfix notation.

What is Infix Notation?

Infix notation is the most commonly used method of writing mathematical expressions, where operators are placed between operands. For example, the expression 2 + 3 is in infix notation.

What is Prefix Notation?

Prefix notation, also known as Polish notation, is a way of writing mathematical expressions where the operator comes before the operands. For example, the expression + 2 3 is in prefix notation.

What is Postfix Notation?

Postfix notation, also known as Reverse Polish notation (RPN), is another way of writing mathematical expressions where the operator comes after the operands. For example, the expression 2 3 + is in postfix notation.

How to Use the Converter
